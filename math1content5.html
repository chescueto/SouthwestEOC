LINEAR FUNCTIONS AND LINEAR INEQUALITIES IN TWO VARIABLES

Tutorial

Linear functions are functions whose graph is a straight line. This is because they have a constant rate.

Linear functions can be written in standard form or slope-intercept form. Standard form is written like ax + by = c, where a, b, and c are all real mnumbers. Slope-intercept is more commonly used, and is written as y = mx + b, where m is the slope and b is the y-intercept.
